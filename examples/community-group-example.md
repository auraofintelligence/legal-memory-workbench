# Community Group Example

This fictional example shows how a community group might prepare context before seeking legal information. It is not legal advice.

## Profile

- Name or alias: Local garden group
- Country: Australia
- State or territory: Queensland
- Council or local government area: Example council
- Purpose: Community group
- Privacy level: Shareable

## Project Summary

The group wants to run monthly working bees and public workshops in a shared garden space.

## Legal Surfaces

- Volunteers and working bee safety.
- Public attendance and accessibility.
- Use of council land or private land.
- Handling contact lists and photos.
- Donations, grant funds or shared purchases.

## Sources To Check

- Local council land use and permit information.
- Volunteer safety guidance.
- Privacy guidance for contact lists and photos.
- Incorporated association or auspice rules, if relevant.

## Plain-English Questions To Explore

- What permission is needed to run public activities in the space?
- What records should be kept for volunteers, incidents and decisions?
- When should the group ask for qualified legal advice?
